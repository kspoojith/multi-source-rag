Indian Education System - Overview
====================================

India has one of the largest education systems in the world with over
1.5 million schools and 50,000 higher education institutions.

Structure:
- Primary Education (Class 1-5): Ages 6-11
- Upper Primary (Class 6-8): Ages 11-14
- Secondary (Class 9-10): Ages 14-16
- Higher Secondary (Class 11-12): Ages 16-18
- Higher Education: Universities and Colleges

Key Policies:
1. Right to Education Act (RTE) 2009: Makes education free and compulsory
   for children aged 6 to 14 years.

2. National Education Policy (NEP) 2020:
   - Replaces the 10+2 structure with 5+3+3+4
   - Emphasis on mother tongue instruction until Class 5
   - Multidisciplinary approach in higher education
   - Target of 50% Gross Enrollment Ratio by 2035
   - Introduction of Academic Bank of Credits

3. Mid-Day Meal Scheme: Provides free lunch to school children,
   covering over 12 crore students.

Top Institutions:
- IITs (Indian Institutes of Technology): 23 across India
- IIMs (Indian Institutes of Management): 20 across India
- AIIMS (All India Institute of Medical Sciences): 22 across India
- Central Universities: 54 across India

Challenges:
- Rural-urban education divide
- Teacher shortage (over 10 lakh vacancies)
- Digital divide in online education
- Low spending (around 3% of GDP vs recommended 6%)
- Quality of education varies significantly across states
