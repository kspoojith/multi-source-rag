Indian Space Programme - ISRO
==============================

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was established on
15 August 1969. Its headquarters is in Bengaluru, Karnataka.
The founder of the Indian space programme is Dr. Vikram Sarabhai.

ISRO ka headquarter Bengaluru mein hai aur yeh Bharat ki space agency hai.
Dr. Vikram Sarabhai ko Indian space programme ka father maana jaata hai.

Key Missions:
1. Chandrayaan-1 (2008): India's first lunar mission
   - Discovered water molecules on the Moon's surface
   - Moon Impact Probe landed near the lunar south pole

2. Mars Orbiter Mission / Mangalyaan (2013):
   - India became the first Asian nation to reach Mars orbit
   - First nation to succeed on its first attempt
   - Cost: Only ₹450 crore ($74 million) - cheapest Mars mission ever
   - Mangalyaan duniya ka sabse sasta Mars mission tha

3. Chandrayaan-2 (2019):
   - Orbiter, lander (Vikram), and rover (Pragyan)
   - Orbiter continues to function and send data
   - Lander had a hard landing near the south pole

4. Chandrayaan-3 (2023):
   - Successfully landed on the lunar south pole on 23 August 2023
   - India became the 4th country to land on the Moon
   - First country to land near the lunar south pole
   - Rover Pragyan operated for 14 days
   - Chandrayaan-3 ne Bharat ko chand par pahunchane wala chautha desh bana diya

5. Aditya-L1 (2023):
   - India's first solar observation mission
   - Placed at Lagrange Point 1 (L1)

Launch Vehicles:
- PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle): Workhorse of ISRO
  - Has launched over 350 satellites including foreign satellites
- GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle): For heavier payloads
- LVM3 (Launch Vehicle Mark-3): For Chandrayaan and Gaganyaan missions

Gaganyaan Mission:
India's first human spaceflight programme. Aims to send 3 Indian astronauts
to low Earth orbit for 3 days. Expected launch in 2025.
Gaganyaan Bharat ka pehla manned space mission hoga.

Budget:
ISRO's annual budget is approximately ₹13,000 crore (about $1.5 billion),
which is significantly less than NASA ($25 billion) or ESA ($7 billion).
Despite the limited budget, ISRO has achieved remarkable success.
